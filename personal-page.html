Pinhole Cameras

One of my hobbies is pinhole photography. I love pinholes because they reduce photography to its basic elements: a hole/aperature, a reactive medium (paper or film), and exposure time. I am facinated by light and darkness, controlling both aspects to make an image is frustrating, exciting, and very rewarding. Here is a 4x5 camera that I made from a cigar-box, a satefy pin, and some scraps from around the house.

A Paper Negative Print from the Cigarbox Pinhole Camera

And here is an photo that I took with the camera, using expired lithography film. Although the exposure and composition are off, I liked the disorienting feel of the photo, with its mix of organic forms, the dark stretching branches from the trees, and manmade architecture, the sprawling brick walls; all of which are encased in a strange swirl of tangled ivory and mishapen plant forms. I also love the way the sunlight sprays down like rain, breaking up the image with spurts and spatters of white space. The image also captures how I felt the day after a long night of drinking, the morning after the art show where my friend Xavi turned an entire room into a camera and then made a dramatic contact print, blasting loud music to stir the chemicals, dancing frantically to shake them into motion, giving us a fantastic show and lots of free wine.
